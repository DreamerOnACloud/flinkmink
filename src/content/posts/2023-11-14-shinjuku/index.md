Utterly jetlagged, we arrived in Tokyo airport; service was super fast; in less than 1 hour we were cleared of customs and immigration, picked up Wi-Fi pocket device and got cash & transport card 🤌

Took Monorail + yamanote lines to arrive at shinkuju. Smooth ride, felt a bit like a Rollercoaster with the train in the air between buildings, turning sideways.
Faye, to her amazement, spotted the mount Fuji from the Monorail, in full glory with snowy peak against a clear blue sky🗻

Arrived at the hotel way too early , 4 hours in advance , had to entertain ourselves until 13:00 for check-in .

Finding breakfast was a necessity, so we didn't mind, had some delicious ramen, but afterwards we were starting to collapse.
Found shinjuku chuo park, and we lied down for while, listening to someone playing the flute in an otherwise serene and quiet setting.

On our walk back to the hotel we came upon a nice surprize. A shrine where people, on a break from the bustle of the workday, would offer a little prayer - 2 claps and a bow 👏👏🙇

We took in the architecture of the shrine, and the oasis of tranquility it offered against a busy city, then headed for the check-in. At last.

Took a coma nap, to recharge our 0% battery and fought a hard battle to wake up. We emerged victorious, and so headed off to discover a tradinional diner resto.

After a series of tasty dishes and a belly full of happiness, we explored the interesting, strange and entertaining kabukichō district.

Entered a brilliant arcade, full of coin vending machines of all sorts.
Faye's attention went to a cat eye-mask and so we inserted some coins in excitement to get it.
Much to our dismay, the machine got stuck and no mask came out.
A Japanese young fella came to the rescue, trying out buttons and frantically adding in his OWN coins to the machine, only to have them greedily swallowed too..
Thankfully, another young man, an employee solved the issue.
We got our mask.
We returned the extra coins to the young fella.
Young fella gave us macha-flavored kit-kats in return 🍫
Amazing ✨
We were surprised by the friendliness and generosity. Arigato gozaimas 🙏🏼
