Woke up at 06:00 to prepare for morning prayer.
We joined the monks in their temple. To enter the holy temple, we first had to purify our hands with a special green powder. Then we entered to take a seat. Dim light from lanterns all over the ceiling and an altar with the hidden buddha, decorated with golden trinkets and a miniature of a pagoda. These monks follow a secular Buddhist religion, and the buddha image is always hidden in a holy box, never displayed. It's opened once a year only to be cleaned by a monk.
Cold, we are seated a long with the other dozen guests on small chairs just a few meters away from the monks.
Monks take their's places in front of the holy buddha, one to the left and one to the right in front of us. They begin the ritual, and towards the end we join in the chanting. The rest of the details shall remain a secret, according to the secular ways of the temple.

(We later found out that the master monk of the temple we were staying was the master of them all, awarded best monk of his class by his master after graduating)

Afterwards, breakfast. Not much, but quality vegetarian (vegan, besides the yogurt) ingredients. Time to visit a couple of main temples around the area and bid then monks farewell 📿👋

After we were done exploring the temples and a couple souvenir shops, Kostas realized he didn't have the camera on him..! Shock, stress and panic ensued.
But we did our best to keep our cool and started retracing our steps in hopes of retrieving the camera.
We looked around thr temples, the bench at the bus stop and in a souvenir shop, but to no avail. Kostas showed a "I lost my Canon camera" phrase to the shop lady in Japanese, and she wondered out loud if anyone from the staff had seen it. They had not.. At that moment, a customer spoke up (in japanese), all I understood was "canon camera" and then he waved at me, signaling me to follow him. He brought me to another store where the friendly staff had safeguarded the camera for me and handed it with a smile. What a relief..The camera has been found! Thank you, Japanese heroes.

While waiting on the hourly bus to take us to the train station, we were approached by a friendly lady, inviting us to a little musician concert in a temple just around the corner. We had 20 minutes to kill, so we thought yeah why not. It was live, by 2 performers, a man with a drum and vocals and a woman with a piano and vocals.
Not the best performance ever, but entertaining enough for 10 minutes.

Then a bus, a cable car, a train (and a little nap) later and voilà, we're in Osaka.
We had a very early morning and very little caffeine since we came to Japan, so we looked for a coffee place. Found a cute one by the river (europe vibes), and had 2 cappuccinos each, to make sure we wake up. Exquisite coffee! ☕☕ and yummy nut and berry cake.

Then we headed for Abeno Harukas, one of the biggest malls in Osaka. We saw street performers performing j-pop songs  in front of the mall. We explored about 7 flours of mall shops.
For food, we spotted a sushi conveyor belt stall in the mall and ate up a dozen little sushi dishes 🍣🍣🍣 After selecting a dish via a tablet screen menu in front of us, it was sent to us via the belt on a tray and we'd pick it up, then press a button to confirm.

After Kostas almost forgetting the backpack this time, which was brought to him by a friendly Japanese person, we decided we should call it a day.
